### [Organic Matter](#om-testing)
      
Note that in this report the data is now represented as the median.  Prior reports represented the average. Switching to the median may give you a better idea of what is typical in your data.  It is not thrown off by any extreme values that might be skewing the overall picture.  I did calculate the averages for a comparison and they are very similar and have included them below for your reference.   

The  median OM measurements can be summarised as follows: 

- Comparing the **GREEN** samples taken on September 18th, 2024 to their most recent previous samples (October 11th, 2023).
  * At a depth of **0-2 cm**, the OM content increased from 9.32% to 9.98%. 
  * At a depth of **2-4 cm**, the OM content increased from 3.68% to 4.46%. 
  * At a depth of **4-6 cm**, the OM content increased from 1.33% to 1.34%.
- Comparing the **TEE** samples taken on September 18th, 2024 to their most recent previous samples (October 11th, 2023).
  * At a depth of **0-2 cm**, the OM content increased from 8.26% to 9.79%. 
  * At a depth of **2-4 cm**, the OM content decreased from 4.6% to 4.39%. 
  * At a depth of **4-6 cm**, the OM content decreased from 4.42% to 3.81%.

*The results show median values.
The median value is the central point of the data, meaning half of the numbers in a list are smaller and half are larger than the median.*

The  average OM measurements can be summarised as follows: 

- Comparing the **GREEN** samples taken on September 18th, 2024 to their most recent previous samples (October 11th, 2023).
  * At a depth of **0-2 cm**, the OM content increased from 8.83% to 9.36%. 
  * At a depth of **2-4 cm**, the OM content increased from 3.84% to 4.44%. 
  * At a depth of **4-6 cm**, the OM content increased from 1.29% to 1.38%.
- Comparing the **TEE** samples taken on September 18th, 2024 to their most recent previous samples (October 11th, 2023).
  * At a depth of **0-2 cm**, the OM content increased from 7.01% to 7.96%. 
  * At a depth of **2-4 cm**, the OM content decreased from 4.41% to 4.18%. 
  * At a depth of **4-6 cm**, the OM content decreased from 3.62% to 3.33%.

Like calibrating a TDR measurement for soil moisture content based on subjective observations, the OM246 test should be thought of in the same context. Playability and overall green performance should be considered.
Using a known amount of topdressing applied, we can estimate/calculate the Amount of topdressing needed to maintain, increase, or decrease the organic matter.

- Consider obtaining an accurate topdressing rate per year to make this calculation possible. What is the estimated depth of all topdressing applications applied this year?
- [This video from the USGA](https://www.asianturfgrass.com/post/sand-topdressing-by-depth) shows how to calculate the depth along with the required conversion equations.
- Cultural decisions such as aerification, verticutting, topdressing amounts/frequency can be influenced by tracking organic matter over time and established goals for organic matter targets.
